Lab Openings

JW Lab (Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Southern University of Science and Technology) is dedicated to the research of optoelectronic information, topological photonics, integrated photonic devices and metamaterials/nanophotonics. We are currently recruiting outstanding researchers, postdoctoral fellows, doctoral students and master students to join our team. We provide a competitive salary, excellent research conditions and a broad academic exchange platform.

Open Positions

Postdoctoral Fellow
Research directions: topological photonics, integrated photonic devices, metamaterials/nanophotonics, optoelectronic information. Responsible for independent research project design, experimental implementation and paper publication, and participate in the application of scientific research projects with the research team.
Ph.D. Student
Research directions: topological photonics, silicon-based integrated photonics, photonic metamaterials, low-dimensional material optoelectronic devices. Undertake the research tasks of the laboratory's scientific research projects, complete the experimental testing and data analysis, and publish high-level academic papers on schedule.
Master Student
Research directions: photonic device characterization, topological photonic structure simulation, integrated photonic device fabrication. Assist in the completion of laboratory experiments, data processing and paper writing, and master the basic skills of optoelectronic experiment and simulation analysis.
Research Assistant / Undergraduate Intern
Responsible for assisting researchers in experimental operation, sample preparation, equipment maintenance and data sorting. Those with basic knowledge of optics, electronics or materials science are preferred; interns can obtain academic guidance and experimental training opportunities.

Application Requirements

  • Have a doctoral/master/bachelor degree in Electronic Engineering, Optics, Materials Science, Physics or related majors (fresh graduates are welcome).
  • Have research experience in topological photonics, integrated photonics, metamaterials, optoelectronic devices or related fields is preferred.
  • Proficient in using optical simulation software (FDTD, Lumerical, COMSOL, etc.) or experimental testing of photonic devices is an advantage.
  • Have good English writing and communication skills, and be able to independently complete the writing and revision of academic papers.
  • Have a rigorous scientific attitude, strong hands-on ability and team cooperation spirit, and be able to actively participate in the research work of the laboratory.

How to Apply

  • Please send the application materials to the laboratory email in PDF format, and the email subject should be marked as 「Application-XXX Position-Your Name」.
  • Application materials include: personal resume (with personal photo, education experience, research experience, published papers, awards, etc.), academic transcript, representative paper/patent/experimental report (if any), and contact information of two recommenders (optional).
  • The laboratory will review the application materials in a rolling manner and invite qualified candidates to interview (online/on-site). The position is open all year round until it is filled.
  • We provide competitive salary and benefits according to the candidate's academic background and research experience, and support academic exchanges and project application.
